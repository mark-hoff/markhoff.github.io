Research

My work explores language variation at the intersection of morphosyntax and pragmatics, primarily in Spanish, Portuguese, and Italian (and occasionally English and French). I adopt quantitative, usage-based approaches (including, increasingly, Construction Grammar) to uncover and make sense of the contextual conditioning behind speakers' choices of linguistic forms.

Lately, I have a particular interest in the qualitative description of previously unstudied pragmatic markers common among young(ish) Argentines. These forms provide valuable insights into areas of study such as negation, polyphony, common ground, etc.
